Thermal study of ECU design

A Finite Element Thermal Analysis of an ECU package unit is done under this project using ANSYS AIM 19.1. As ECU contains power electronic devices they tends to generate a lot of heat and if this heat is trapped inside, it can cause damage to the internal components of the ECU due to high temperature rise. In this project the method of Air cooling is used to keep the ECU under it's maximum working Temperature range. The effect of different fins design on the maximum internal temperature is studied.

The outer Air surrounds the entire ECU package. It is given to simulate the effect of atmospheric air cooling

Boundry conditions

Following are the given boundry conditions -

  • 11 chips (uC) that are mounted on the PCB are considered the main source of heat generation. 30W as solid heat source is given in total to all the chips combined together.
  • The base of the ECU is given 105 degree C temperature to represent the ambient temp. condition.
  • Outer volume (air) two faces are given as inlet and outlet
  • Walls surrounding the outer air are insulated wall boundaries

Mesh study

In this project, there are different geometries because the designs of the Fins are changed to obtain better thermal properties, but the design changes are only on the top section of the geometry i.e. fins, all other geometry parts remain the same because of this reason instead of performing mesh study individually on all different design, the study is done only for one design to find the best mesh settings and these same settings are then applied to all other designs.The mesh was refiened using several steps and in each step, refinement was stopped when the difference in maximum temperature was less then 2%. These steps are as following-

  • Step 1: In this step the mesh settings are applied as base settings which are by default predefined by the software automatically.
  • step 2: In this setup, the mesh boundary layer is defined manually, as the software is not able to generate it automatically while keeping all other settings the same as the previous one.
  • step 3: In this step, the meshing element shapes have been modified, based on the fact that complex geometry can be easily meshed using Tetrahedrons, but this requires more solution time, whereas simple geometries can be meshed using Hexahedrons which requires less solution time. Bodies with simple geometry like multilayer PCB, uC, inner air, base are given Hexahedrons element shape while complex geometries like the cover, outer air are given Tetrahedrons element shape.
  • Step 4: In this setup, the two mesh defining properties body sizing and edge sizing are determined. The approach is that the smaller the size of these elements, the better is the mesh refinement and hence results, but more refine mesh needs more time (computational power) to obtain results, so there should be an appropriate, balanced refinement of mesh. These settings are applied to the bodies which are more dominant and have more effect on high temp. reached within.

Results

DESIGN 1.

Design 1 is the simplest design among all others, with straight rectangle shaped 9 fins attached to the top of the cover. By evaluating the results of design 1 it is observed that the max. temp. reached within the chips is 112,91 degrees and the location where the max and min temperature is observed, can be seen in the figure above.

DESIGN 2.

In this design the fins form a curved path for the air to travel between two consecutive fins. This design has 9 number of fins of unequal lengths. The max. temperature reached in the chips is 110,27.

DESIGN 3.

This design is quite similar to the first design but the fins here are made in two steps. The upper portion of the fins have different width then the base. The max. temp. reached in the chips is 109,53 degrees.

After observing and evaluating the results of all three different design it is inferred that the design 3 has the best affect in reducing the max. temp. within the chips.
